MY WORK

I am outdoor junky, with a huge apetite for tehnology everything what I do is somehow related with nature, physical activity and technology. Since I've moved to Munich I've started to explore Bavaria and Bavarian lakes and alps. Geography may have handed Germanys south a mere sliver of the Alps, but the region certainly makes the most of its peaks, many of them can be reached from Munich by train. Surrounded by Austria on three sides, the Berchtesgadener Land in Bavarias far southeast keeps all the regions clichéd Alpine promises, providing dramatic mountain scenery, great hiking and Germanys prettiest lake, the Königssee. So far I have explored the region from Königssee to Zugspitze, the highest mountain in Germany. I am lucky because I was able to merge my passion for bikes and work. I am working in NextBike, Europe's largest bike sharing company.

Before I have moved to Germany I was active in Croatia and Italy.

In Croatia I have worked as an manager of tourist information center in Riva rafting centar in Obrovac, Croatia. On this position I was responsible for ensuring that the center is well presented, efficiently organized and easy to use I was also in charge for promotion and marketing. Before my engagement in Riva rafting center I was a junior assistant for European funds and investment at the department of investment and European funds in Karlovac municipality. My tasks were monitoring of tenders, project management, writing project proposals and managing department administration.

In 2014 I was a representative of Karlovac County in Italywhere I participated in Eurodyssee program. My internship was in tourism company Rafting Umbria . During my staying in Italy I experienced different culture and also besides English I am able to have a basic communication in Italian. Since 2014 I am actively involved in Erasmus + program. Through this program I participated in training courses in Slovakia, Italy, Poland and Turkey. On training courses I gained new skills in area of communication and also I learned to think “out of the box”.
